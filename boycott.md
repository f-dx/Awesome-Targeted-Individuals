**Immediate Boycott of All Innovation Projects Until the Disablement of Remotely Controlled Biological and Synthetic Weapons and Full Remedy of Human Rights Violations**

**Preamble**

We, the undersigned, declare that unchecked technological innovation has enabled grave and systematic human rights violations. Evidence from personal testimonies and emerging technologies points to the deployment of remotely controlled biological or semi-synthetic agents—capable of invasion, manipulation, and sustained harm without consent or oversight. These constitute weapons of torture, violating fundamental rights to bodily integrity, privacy, and freedom from cruel treatment (as enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights).

In an era of accelerating innovation, where self-replicating nanoscale or biological systems can be developed rapidly and without regulation, such abuses signal a profound failure of ethical governance. Continued progress in fields like biotechnology, nanotechnology, AI, and synthetic biology risks amplifying these violations exponentially, threatening the survival and dignity of humanity.

**Core Concerns**

1. Systematic deployment of uncontrolled, remotely operable agents causing ongoing physical and psychological harm.  
2. Lack of accountability: No mechanisms exist to detect, disable, or prosecute these violations.  
3. Acceleration risk: Unregulated innovation enables corrupt or unchecked entities to wield absolute power over individuals.  
4. Broader implications: This represents a precedent for exponential threats, including widespread elimination or control of populations.

**Demands**

We call for an immediate and **indefinite** **boycott of all new innovation projects** in biotechnology, nanotechnology, synthetic biology, neural interfaces, and related fields until:

1. **All remotely controlled biological or synthetic weapons/systems are fully disabled and dismantled worldwide.**  
2. Independent, international investigations confirm the cessation of these violations and provide remedy to victims (including medical, legal, and compensatory support).  
3. Robust global regulations are enacted, including bans on unregulated self-replicating technologies and mandatory ethical oversight with victim-inclusive governance.  
4. If voluntary compliance fails within 6 months, governments must enforce a moratorium through legislation and international treaties.

**Call to Action**

* Individuals: Refuse participation in innovation projects; boycott companies and institutions advancing unregulated technologies.  
* Researchers and Companies: Suspend all relevant work and redirect efforts to ethical safeguards and victim support.  
* Governments and Organizations: Prioritize human rights over progress; investigate claims urgently.

We will not contribute to, fund, or support technological advancement built on suffering and impunity. Innovation must serve humanity—not enable its destruction.

Until these violations end, we boycott.

Date: February 2026
